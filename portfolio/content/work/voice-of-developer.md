## The goal

A platform with millions of developers hears from a tiny, unrepresentative fraction of them. Roadmaps dictated by the loudest enterprise voices systematically ignore the long tail — and the long tail is where platform growth compounds.

## What shipped

I created and led an AI-driven Voice of Developer program: a 360° pipeline that ingests unstructured developer signals at scale. I wired Discord, Stack Overflow, GitHub issues, and support tickets into a system that outputs ranked, evidence-backed roadmap priorities. By combining this with strategic customer input and org-wide dogfood events, I grounded our product bets in actual global developer friction, not recency bias from the last escalation.

I built this as a direct descendant of the Voice of Customer program I created at Mapbox — which previously spanned enterprise customers, SI partners, and scaled developers to directly produce five-plus new products. I rebuilt the motion for a platform ten times the size, using AI to do the reading.

## Why it matters

Working backwards from growth demands an honest picture of demand. This program serves as the demand-sensing layer. It isolates the friction silently capping adoption, sizes the impact, and hands it to product and engineering backed by hard receipts.
