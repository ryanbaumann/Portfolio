Engineer, builder, tinkerer — and a product and engineering leader who has spent 15+ years scaling developer platforms at Google, Mapbox, and Instabase. I set the strategy, and I stay in the work: I dogfood the products, review traces, write evals, ship reference apps, read the transcripts, and show up exactly where developers are. The proof lives on this site — [the lab](/demos/) is a set of live apps I build and operate myself.

My operating thesis: **developer experience is a growth discipline.** The platforms that win do not just write better docs — they work backwards from hard adoption and revenue goals to drive the product, context, distribution, and ecosystem investments that move metrics. This holds true whether the platform sells maps, document AI, or anything else.

## Google — 2021 to present

**Head of Developer Experience, Google Maps Platform** (2022 – present). I lead Developer Experience Engineering for a platform powering 10M+ websites and apps. I direct a team spanning engineering, product management, technical writing, and developer relations with a multi-million dollar budget, operating as a single integrated unit with Product and Engineering.

Our mission is making AI agents first-class users of the platform: docs-driven development, agent-ready documentation, and evals that optimize platform context for strict outcomes. I drove the headline launches — [Code Assist](https://developers.google.com/maps/ai/code-assist), [agent skills](https://github.com/googlemaps/agent-skills), the agentic eval suite, Agent Engine Optimization, and AI-native distribution into AI Studio, Replit, and Lovable — all detailed in [Work](/work/).

**Manufacturing Industry Solutions Manager, Google Cloud** (2021 – 2022). I led 0→1 product and engineering for Google Cloud's first manufacturing industry solution, [Intelligent Product Essentials](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/data-analytics/introducing-intelligent-products-essentials-for-manufacturers). I shipped edge AI and cloud analytics for connected products, launching with GE Appliances in nine months and securing a $10M+ launch pipeline.

## Mapbox — 2016 to 2020

I joined as the first customer-facing engineer at Series B. I scaled the team from 1 to 15 through Series D as the company grew past $100M ARR.

**Senior Manager, Solutions Architecture** (2017 – 2020). I built the platform-wide Voice of Customer program spanning enterprise customers, SI partners, and scaled developers — directly producing five-plus new products, including [Movement](https://docs.mapbox.com/data/movement/guides/). I led the [OSS partnership with Uber](https://blog.mapbox.com/launching-custom-layers-with-uber-2a235841a125) that established Mapbox as the default basemap in deck.gl and kepler.gl. I shipped packaged solutions — [Asset Tracking](https://www.mapbox.com/solutions/asset-tracking/), [Interactive Storytelling](https://blog.mapbox.com/8-compelling-stories-told-with-the-storytelling-solution-3d33ab7b34e1) — with partners like AWS, converting 1:1 consulting engagements into a repeatable go-to-market engine.

**Senior Solutions Architect** (2016 – 2017). I operated as the 0→1 product manager for [Boundaries](https://www.mapbox.com/boundaries) and [Atlas](https://www.mapbox.com/atlas). I drove strategy, build, and launch, securing the first $5M ARR before handing off to dedicated PMs. Both remain in the core portfolio 8+ years later. I also shipped [mapboxgl-jupyter](https://github.com/mapbox/mapboxgl-jupyter) and [mapboxgl-powerbi](https://github.com/mapbox/mapboxgl-powerbi), unlocking the data science and BI communities for the platform.
